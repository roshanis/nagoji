## POV and voice

### What holds

- First person contract is consistent across the manuscript: battles, court scenes, and domestic scenes stay anchored in Nagoji’s senses, access, and inference.
- The memoir texture (occasional hindsight, “Only later…”) generally feels earned because Nagoji is positioned close to power and paperwork.

### Final read-through flags

- A few places use **third person self-naming** for identity pivot effect:
  - `book3_chapter17_adoption_of_the_stranger.md:161` (“Nagoji Sawant had… / Ananthan Pillai would…”)
  - `book3_chapter19_shadows_of_the_deccan.md:285` (“Nagoji Sawant had died…”)  
  This can work as a deliberate stylistic knife turn, but it is one of the only places where the narration briefly stops saying “I”. Decide whether you want that effect, and if not, convert those lines to first person.
